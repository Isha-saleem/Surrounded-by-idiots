Red: The Dominant

Direct, decisive, and driven. They want to win and they want it done now.

Yellow: The Inspiring

Enthusiastic, creative, and persuasive. They love being the center of attention.

Green: The Stable

Kind, patient, and consistent. They value the group over themselves.

Blue: The Analytical

Correct, cautious, and detail-oriented. They believe in quality and logic.

Strengths & Weaknesses

THE RED PERSONALITY
Strengths
  • • High-speed decision making
  • • Natural leadership and initiative
  • • Extremely goal-oriented
  • • Courageous in the face of conflict
  • • Efficient and productivity-focused
Weaknesses
  • • Can be perceived as arrogant
  • • Poor listener when in a hurry
  • • Little patience for small talk
  • • May prioritize goals over people
THE YELLOW PERSONALITY
Strengths
  • • Highly creative and full of ideas
  • • Excellent communication
  • • Contagious energy and optimism
  • • Great at networking
  • • Adaptable and quick to start
Weaknesses
  • • Often disorganized or forgetful
  • • Struggles with follow-through
  • • Can talk too much
  • • Prioritizes being liked over truth
THE GREEN PERSONALITY
Strengths
  • • Reliable and extremely dependable
  • • Calm under pressure and patient
  • • Great team players
  • • Empathetic and wonderful listeners
  • • Consistent and loyal
Weaknesses
  • • Resistant to change
  • • Avoids conflict at all costs
  • • Can be overly passive
  • • Struggles with independent decisions
THE BLUE PERSONALITY
Strengths
  • • Highly logical and objective
  • • Incredible attention to detail
  • • Systematic and organized
  • • High standards for quality
  • • Think before they speak
Weaknesses
  • • Overly critical of self and others
  • • Prone to analysis paralysis
  • • Perceived as cold or detached
  • • Gets lost in the details

Communication Strategy

R

Working with Reds

Challenge: They may seem impatient or aggressive.

  • Be Direct: Get to the point.
  • Results: Focus on the outcome.
  • Confidence: Stand your ground.
Y

Working with Yellows

Challenge: They can be disorganized or talkative.

  • Social: Chat before business.
  • Writing: Summarize agreements.
  • Praise: Show appreciation.
G

Working with Greens

Challenge: They hate sudden change or conflict.

  • Time: Don't rush big decisions.
  • Connection: Build genuine rapport.
  • Safe: Encourage them to speak.
B

Working with Blues

Challenge: They need massive amounts of data.

  • Data: Bring facts and research.
  • Accuracy: No empty flattery.
  • Structure: Stick to the agenda.
